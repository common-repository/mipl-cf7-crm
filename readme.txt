Integrate your "Contact Form 7" with a CRM system to automatically capture leads from your website.


== Description ==

"MIPL CF7 CRM" is a WordPress plugin designed to seamlessly integrate your chosen CRM system with "Contact Form 7" plugin. This plugin simplifies the process of capturing and managing leads generated from your website's contact forms.


= Features =

1. *CRM Compatibility:* The plugin is compatible with a range of CRM systems, making it versatile and adaptable to your specific CRM choice.

2. *Custom Field Mapping:* Customize and map the form fields in "Contact Form 7" to your CRM's fields, ensuring that the data collected aligns perfectly with your CRM's structure.

3. *Effortless Data Transfer:* "MIPL CF7 CRM" automates the process of transferring data submitted through your contact forms directly into your CRM system. This minimizes manual data entry and reduces the risk of errors.

4. *Streamlined Lead Management:* Efficiently manage and track leads generated from your website. The plugin helps you organize leads and ensures they are promptly and accurately added to your CRM.

5. *Automated email notifications:* Receive real-time notifications or trigger automated actions in your CRM based on form submissions, enhancing your ability to respond to leads promptly.

6. *RESP API support:* Leverage the power of RESP API-based CRMs for smooth and secure data exchange.

7. *Flexible authentication options*: Choose from a variety of authentication methods, including OAuth 2.0, API keys, and more, to suit your specific CRM requirements.


By using "MIPL CF7 CRM," you can improve the efficiency of your lead generation and customer relationship management efforts. This plugin offers a valuable solution for integrating CRM into your website's contact forms.


== Installation ==

1. Upload plugins folder to the `/wp-content/plugins/` directory.
2. Activate the plugin through the 'Plugins' menu in WordPress.
3. Configure form fields: Customize the form to gather the data you need.
4. Configure the integration: map form fields to CRM fields, and specify CRM lists or segments.
5. Test the integration: Verify that data from the contact form flows into your CRM correctly.
